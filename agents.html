Business Space · flagship agent

EagleBot — a dev-team member in your company Space.

EagleBot lives in your company's Slack and works like a colleague. Ask it anything about your product and it answers from your own knowledge — specs, Notion, analytics, designs. Assign it a task and it triages the work into a reviewed development plan. Approve the plan and it ships the pull request: isolated specialist sessions write the code in clean workspaces, open the PR, and report back in the thread — with every step traced and costed. Born inside GitLaw, where it runs the engineering team's day-to-day in production.

What it does

  • Answers from company knowledge — docs, tickets, analytics, Figma — in the thread where you asked.
  • Triages tasks into plans — assign a ticket; it gathers context, classifies the work, and drafts a development plan for review.
  • Ships real PRs — delegates to specialist sessions that write code in isolated workspaces and open the pull request.
  • Shows its work — every query traced: tools used, agents spawned, tokens spent.

How it fits by9am

  • Lives in your Business Space — reads and writes the Space's memory; your Personal and Family Spaces stay invisible to it.
  • Scoped like any agent — you grant exactly the tools and repos it may touch, nothing more.
  • Drafts, never fires — plans wait for your sign-off and PRs wait for your review. Nothing merges itself.
  • Same one mind — the orchestrator delegates to it like every other agent, so its work lands in your morning brief.

How we position it

  • A team member, not a tool — priced per seat on the Business tier, because that's what it replaces.
  • Proven, not promised — battle-tested in production at GitLaw before it ever became a product.
  • Sovereign by design — your codebase, tickets and docs stay in your single-tenant Space. No pooled SaaS graph ever sees them.
  • The reason to open a Business Space — land Personal, expand to the company.
